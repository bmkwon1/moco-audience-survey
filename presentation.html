Target Audience Motivations
What mo.co can deliver
MotivationWhat the research says
Flexible Social Play68% of 16-24 year olds prefer co-op over solo play (MIDiA, 2024). 58% of Gen Z consider gaming their primary social space.
Self-ExpressionDesign is one of the most gender-inclusive motivations in * Quantic Foundry's model. 84% of Roblox Gen Z users draw real-life fashion inspiration from avatar customization.
Skill Mastery without PunishmentChallenge motivation is distinct from Competition in * Quantic Foundry's model. Players can score high on Challenge and low on Competition simultaneously.
Variety and FreshnessYounger generations play an average of 6 genres vs. 2-3 for older players (Newzoo, 2023). Novelty is the #1 driver of reactivation and social conversation.
Our bet: Monster Hunter: World (21M+ copies), Helldivers 2 (20M+ copies, $700M+ revenue), Deep Rock Galactic (8M+ copies), and Warframe (50M+ registered players) all proved this market exists at scale on PC/console. On Steam, co-op PvE titles are just 5% of releases but 46% of all copies sold (VG Insights, 2024). Mobile has no dominant equivalent.
Scope
Who mo.co is not for
Pure PvP Dominators
Want zero-sum outcomes and ranking superiority over others. * Quantic Foundry "Gladiator"/"Ninja" types. High Competition motivation.
Hyper-Casual One-Tap
Want zero learning curve, no skill ceiling. * Quantic Foundry "Gardener" type. Would churn once novelty fades.
Hardcore Scheduled Raiders
Require planned group sessions, long-form obligation, guild structures. Fundamentally incompatible with drop-in design.
Narrative-First Solo
Primarily motivated by story and authored world-building. * Quantic Foundry "Slayer"/"Architect" types. mo.co's PvE is systems-driven, not narrative-driven.
These exclusions are about primary motivation, not demographics. A 25-year-old who loves Elden Ring can still be a mo.co player if their primary driver is self-improvement, not competitive rank.
Motivational Profile
The player we are building for
"Players who want freedom in how they play, to get better at something real, express themselves in the process, and share that experience with others; without the intensity or pressure of PvP."
Framework
Segmentation Design Principles
Motivation-first, not demographic-first
Age is a tendency signal, not a segment boundary. Each cluster has observable behavioral proxies.
Behaviorally observable
Every cluster maps to measurable in-game and survey behaviors, enabling real player identification.
Mutually distinct
Clusters are separated by primary motivation axis, not tone. Avoids false overlap.
Quantifiable
Cluster size estimates anchored to external research, not internal assumption.
Competitive advantage
mo.co's features and content should give structural advantages over other games with these players.

Self-Directed Socials"I can play how I want, but I'm never alone."1 / 4
Who they are
  • Play frequently, entirely on their own terms — no schedules, no commitments
  • Comfortable being "alone together": same space as others without coordination pressure
  • Social through presence and ambient awareness, not voice chat or planning
  • Likely active across multiple games simultaneously
Why they fit mo.co
  • Drop-in/drop-out multiplayer directly serves their playstyle
  • Cooperative PvE removes social anxiety from playing with strangers
  • Open areas and shared world content deliver the "alone together" feeling
  • Skill matters, but failure is never humiliating — it's against the environment

Expressive Explorers"This game lets me show who I am, not just how good I am."1 / 4
Who they are
  • Highly attuned to aesthetics, vibe, and visual identity — in-game and on social media
  • Switches builds, weapons, and loadouts frequently; variety is how they explore self
  • Shares content: clips, screenshots, loadouts, cosmetics — being seen is part of the game
  • Wants to express personality, gender, and taste without being judged on performance
Why they fit mo.co
  • PvE removes meta-anxiety: experiment with builds freely, no ranked consequences
  • Cooperative play creates shareable moments, not just personal wins
  • Visual rewards, skins, and weapon aesthetics have direct social currency
  • Variety in content (modes, monsters, events) feeds exploration and switching behavior
Key emotional payoff
"When I log in, I'm working on something that's mine. Other players see it. It matters."

Skillful Achievers"I'm getting better and it feels good, not stressful."1 / 4
Who they are
  • Motivated by improvement, learning systems, optimizing play, reading enemies
  • Dislikes hardcore competitive culture and the emotional cost of losing to another person
  • Wants to feel competent and capable, not dominant or superior over others
  • Self-directed: sets their own benchmarks for success
Why they fit mo.co
  • PvE provides safe skill expression: failure is against the environment, not a person
  • Hard/Nightmare modes and Trophy Leagues give them a personal ceiling to chase
  • Co-op PvE rewards competence (carrying your team, landing perfect dodges) without humiliating anyone
  • Seasonal mastery tracks let them demonstrate improvement over time

  • Seeks out higher difficulty modes voluntarily
  • Checks their own performance stats or rankings
  • Avoids or abandons games with punishing PvP rank loss
  • Values challenge that feels fair, not cheap or random
